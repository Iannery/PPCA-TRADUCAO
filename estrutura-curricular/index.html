Curricular Structure

 Core Subjects
 Name Summary
Data Structures and Algorithms Provide a formal and systematic overview of the basic concepts of computer models, formal languages, and algorithms. Introduce the concept of data structures. Introduce the main linear and non-linear data structures and discuss their applications. Introduce the main algorithms for sorting and manipulating data structures. 
Database Fundamentals Fundamental database concepts. Data models and languages. Database design. Components of a database system: query processing, disaster recovery, and concurrency control.
Statistical Analysis of Data and Information Descriptive analysis, classification, analysis, and interpretation of univariate and multivariate information, Box-Plot models and techniques, and evaluation of outliers. Analysis of continuous and discrete random variables. Notions on sampling, position, and dispersion measures. Probability, main discrete and continuous distributions, statistical inference, and confidence intervals. Hypothesis testing and non-parametric tests. Models of variance analysis, F-test, and Chi-square. Regression and Correlation.
Data Mining Conceptualization of data and text mining. CRISP-DM reference model. Techniques for data preprocessing. Data mining algorithms. Evaluation methods. Data mining tools. Case Studies.
Fundamentals of Operations Research Conceptualization of operations research. Basic concepts of linear programming, non-linear programming, stochastic processes, queue theory, and simulation.
Project Management Project management; risk management; project organization; project control, measurement, and monitoring; project review and evaluation (customer satisfaction, performance, etc.); alignment of IT infrastructure with the organization; project management techniques (PMBOK, SCRUM, etc.); IT governance.

 

Software Engineering Subjects
Name Summary
Requirement Engineering Fundamentals of requirement engineering; development constraints, functional and non-functional requirements; requirement engineering processes (scope definition, identification, elicitation, specification, evaluation, and traceability of requirements); notations for requirement specification (use cases and user stories); formal techniques for requirement specification.
Software Architecture and Design Performance tactics, security, availability, maintainability, and other quality attributes; architectural views; architectural styles and patterns (layers, MVC, publish-subscribe, microservices, peer-to-peer); cloud computing and containerization; structural and behavioral diagrams; principles and patterns for microservices design.
Software Construction Fundamentals of software construction (coupling, change anticipation, verification, and testing); main decisions related to construction; building code with quality (detailed software design, defensive programming); secure code construction; code improvement and refactoring; test-driven development.

Software Verification, Validation, and Testing

 Fundamentals of verification, validation, and testing processes; verification and validation quality techniques (inspection, peer review, audits); testing and static quality analysis; testing levels (unit, integration, system, and acceptance); testing techniques (specification-based, code-based, fault-based models, etc.); quality models and characteristics; software quality and maturity standards (MPS.BR, ISO 9126, CMMI); software quality assurance; quality of testing artifacts.

Advanced Topics in Software Engineering

 Current topics of interest in research and development in Software Engineering.
Research in Software Engineering Methodological foundations for thesis preparation. Scientific guidance. Supervision and support from the advisor for the student's work plan development.

 

Risk Management Subjects
Name Summary
Governance In this subject, students will learn about the Matrix of form and tangibility of value objects. They will study the value proposition of risk management, ERM (Enterprise Risk Management). The model in question covers two aspects of risk assessment: a) Traditional Assets with evaluation of monetary and physical risk regulation models, and reliability with learning of FTA, FMEA, and ETA (Decision Tree) techniques, and b) Intellectual Capital in risk models, Organization Systems and Processes, and Relationships with Suppliers and Clients. Evaluation of ISO 26000 and ISO 31000 standards.
Physical and Financial Asset Risk Management Macroeconomic concepts: Fiscal, Exchange, and Monetary Policy. Financial market and Capital market. Banking regulation and Operational, Market, and Liquidity Risk. The analytical structure as a cost organizer; direct and indirect costs, fixed and variable; estimates; financial schedule; Trade-offs; time-cost; Crash algorithm; S curve and EVA; added value; cash flow; simple and compound interest; time value of money; present value, future value, equivalent annual benefit; inflation; depreciation and income tax; economic feasibility: certainty and uncertainty and comparison of alternatives.
Decision Making Models and Performance Management Assess the performance of organizations' management through evaluative criteria consistent with the real situation of the studied problem and resulting in a set of numerical solutions that assist in decision making. Enable the construction of evaluative models capable of diagnosing the problem within the organization and presenting quantitative results, using Simulation methodologies and models, Data Envelopment Analysis, and Multi-criteria Decision Analysis Support - MCDA built based on Piaget's epistemological trend and his constructivist vision.

Project Risk Management

 Project management fundamentals: concept, types, life cycle, management processes, stakeholders, project offices, knowledge areas in project management. Project risk management: elaboration, risk management processes, and tools. Change management: dimensions and barriers, understanding needs, creation and implementation of change plan, review of results, and tools.

Advanced Topics in Risk Management

Current topics of interest in research and development in Risk Management.
Research in Risk Management Methodological foundations for thesis preparation. Scientific guidance. Supervision and support from the advisor for the student's work plan development.

 

Data Science Subjects
Name Summary
Intensive Computational Methods for Data Mining Non-parametric methods and robust statistics, statistical inference through simulations: likelihood, Monte Carlo tests and confidence intervals, Bootstrapping, the MCMC (Markov Chain Monte Carlo) method.
Basic Concepts of Sample Selection Probabilistic sampling, stratified sampling, use of auxiliary variables in stratified sampling, systematic sampling, cluster sampling, complex sampling plans, non-response effects: weighting methods for non-response.
Massive Data Mining Conceptualization of massive data analysis, parallel processing applied to massive data analysis, data mining algorithms for massive data analysis, text mining algorithms for massive data analysis, social network analysis algorithms for massive data analysis, tools for massive data analysis, case study.

Massive Databases

 Types of databases, conceptualization of non-relational databases, column-oriented databases, document-oriented databases, graph-oriented databases, key-value-oriented databases.

Advanced Topics in Data Mining

 Current topics of interest in research and development in Data Mining.
Research in Data Mining Methodological foundations for thesis preparation. Scientific guidance. Supervision and support from the advisor for the student's work plan development.
