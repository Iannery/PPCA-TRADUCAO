Applied Computing Postgraduate Program

Masters in Applied Computing

The Masters in Applied Computing (MPCA) program covers the study of algorithms and their implementation, expressed in the form of software to be executed on computers and applied to solve problems in specific fields of knowledge. It aims to train technical, innovative, and entrepreneurial leaders, teaching students how to transform research into practical applications.

The MPCA has a minimum duration of 18 months and a maximum duration of 24 months, with a single yearly entrance. The student's qualification defense must take place by the 12th month, which will include the specification of the Final Project/Thesis to be developed. The Thesis defense must take place by the 24th month. During the 3rd and 4th semesters, the student must present two progress seminars. Exceptionally, with justification accepted by the department, an extension of the deadline for defense can be granted for up to 6 months, with a maximum of 30 months.

The subjects are structured into a basic core and three specialization tracks. Each subject amounts to 2 credits, of which 12 credits from the basic core are mandatory for all specialization programs, and a further 12 credits are mandatory per specialization program. The student must take a total of 24 credits distributed as follows:

- six core subjects, totaling twelve credits, taken in the first semester;
- six specific subjects in each specialization line, totaling twelve credits;
- final work/approved thesis (no credits granted).

 

All classes are held on the Darcy Ribeiro Campus, on the premises of the Computer Science Department and the Informatics Center of the University of Brasilia. The theoretical classes will be held in classrooms equipped with a whiteboard, air conditioning, a multimedia projector, and Internet access via the UnB Wireless network. The practical classes will be taught in laboratory rooms equipped with a whiteboard, air conditioning, 23 notebooks, including one for the professor, a multimedia projector, and Internet access via REDUnB, the University of Brasília's internal network. Classes are held on Fridays, from 8 am to 12:30 pm and from 2 pm to 6:30 pm, with a 30-minute break, and on Saturdays, from 8 am to 12:30 pm, with a 30-minute break.

 

Program Goals

- provide the students with a solid and high-quality theoretical formation by attending the core subjects; 
- provide the students with an updated and high-quality specific formation within the chosen research area; 
- introduce the students to the world of academic and technological research in computer science, developing their methodological and experimental skills; 
- enable the students to apply the assimilated concepts towards the proposition of possible solutions for real problems in their field of work;
- conduct academic or technological research in a joint effort of students, professors, and external collaborators;  
- train IT managers and analysts in the stricto-sensu post-graduate level, to improve their work performance in their respective institutions; and create an index of successful cases originating from the course's conclusion projects/theses.

 

The alumni will be able to carry out scientific and technological investigations focused on problems they face in their professional activities in one of the fields of activity of this program.
