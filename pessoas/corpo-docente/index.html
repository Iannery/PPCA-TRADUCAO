She is currently a professor in the Department of Computer Science at the University of Brasília. She has experience in the field of Computer Science, with an emphasis on Parallel Processing, Distributed Systems, Grid Computing, Combinatorial Optimization, and Metaheuristics, mainly focusing on grid computing, parallel metaheuristics, and parallel and distributed algorithms.

 

Prof. Dr. Edison Ishikawa (Lattes)

Prof. Dr. Jacir Luiz Bordim (Lattes)

Prof. Dr. Marcelo Antonio Marotta (Lattes)

Additionally, I hold a bachelor's degree in Computer Science from the Federal University of Itajubá (UNIFEI), Itajubá, MG, Brazil (2010). Currently, I am researching Cloud-based Radio Access Networks (C-RAN), Next Generation Networks, Future Internet, Business Process Management, Software Defined Networking, Internet of Things, Software Defined Radio, and Cognitive Radio.


Prof. Dr. Marcos Fagundes Caetano (Lattes)

Prof. Dr. João Carlos Félix Souza (Lattes)

Prof. Dr. João Mello da Silva (Lattes)
Holds a degree in Mechanical Engineering from the School of Engineering of São Carlos (1969), a master's degree in Operations Research - Case Western Reserve University (1973), a master's degree in Systems Analysis and Applications from the National Institute for Space Research (1971), and a Ph.D. in Operations Research - Case Western Reserve University (1975). As a professor at the Federal University of São Carlos from 1977 to 1979, he was part of the team that implemented the Production Engineering program at that institution. As an engineer at Telebrás from 1981 to 1998 and at Brasil Telecom from 1998 to 2003, and as a partner-manager at Azevedo Marques Mello Consultancy and Engineering from 2003 to 2008, he worked in the area of Production Engineering of Telecommunications Services, with an emphasis on Telecommunications Services Planning and Business between Telecommunications Service Providers, addressing topics such as business engineering, quality management, business relationships between telecommunications service providers, regulation, and Risk Management. A professor at the Technology College at the University of Brasília since 1979, he is currently the Coordinator of Production Engineering, being part of the team that is implementing the Production Engineering course at UnB. He has been responsible for the Risk Management chair of the Specialization Course in Occupational Health and Safety at UnB. Research areas of interest include Maturity and Value Creation in Production Systems, particularly through Project Management; Risk Management; and Education in Production Engineering, with a special focus on synthesis, integration, and entrepreneurship activities.

Profª. Drª. Simone Borges Simão Monteiro (Lattes)
Holds a degree in Chemical Engineering from the Federal University of Uberlândia (1995), a Master's in Production Engineering (Agroindustrial Systems Management) from the Federal University of São Carlos (1998), a Specialization in Lean Production Systems from USP (2004), and a Ph.D. in Production Engineering (Quality Management) from the Federal University of São Carlos (2006).
